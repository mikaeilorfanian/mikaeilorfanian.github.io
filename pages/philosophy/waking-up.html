Disrupting Dreams

It's easy to get lost in the daily life and think that this era is somehow different. After all, we have all these modern institutions (universities, corporations, military, worldwide unions, giant technology companies) and technology that has made our life vastly better than our predecessor's. It feels like things around you are there for a good reason. That your lifetime is unique and special. All these intelligent people running the world. Their understanding of how things should be run is vastly deeper than yours. Of course, you life every day in the current year and decade, not in the past. People rarely study history these days.
Living in the now leads to a false sense of security or trust in how things are going. You're content with how things are. The education system, sciences, medicine, business, etc. All these institutions are at their most advanced state ever. They attract geniuses. Why would you even bother questioning them?
The truth is that your lifetime is just one of many thousands in the history of mankind. Chances are most of the what we're dealing with isn't unique at all. Many generations have had to deal with these issues and the fact that these issues aren't solved yet means whatever solutions previous generations came up with are not good enough for us.
There's a very good lesson to be learned from the mistakes of the past. You see that it's quite possible for whole populations to be wrong about an important issue. The mistake is so obvious to you now that you pity them. They were chasing their tails; there was no chance they could've succeeded.
It's not like such mass delusions are rare. It seems like every generation has quite a few of them. Once you realize this you begin to wonder what delusions you're indulging in? Equipped with this knowledge, you begin to question things. Things people are totally fine with. They get annoyed. "How stupid are these questions, what's wrong with you today?" they say. This is the expected reaction from someone whose dream has been disrupted. No more sweet dreams for you!
