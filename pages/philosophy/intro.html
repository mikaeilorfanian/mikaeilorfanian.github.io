Intro to Top 1% Developer

Within the next two year, do you think it is possible to become a top 1% developer so you
- make top 1% salary compared to people with your level of experience
- get rated as a top 1% team member by the people you work with
- progress faster than 99% of the people in the industry
- contribute a ton of value to each project you join
Yes, it is! Believe it or not, you can do it without even working harder. Actually, you'd probably end up working less. You see, most of the changes you need to make to become a top 1% developer have to do with how you think or do things rather than what you think/do.
Let me give you an example. Do you think it's possible to get promoted (going from Junior to Regular or Regular to Senior) and earn more money in the next few weeks? You might say "Well, it depends on my skills, am I even qualified for the role? I'll have to learn this or that technology/stack/framework/language!". Wrong! Your skills have little to do with your title. The answer to that question is "yes" and here's how you can do it: apply for jobs that offer the role you want and eventually you'll land one. When you get that job, you'll automatically jump to the top 1% of earners with your level of experience since you're earning "senior" salary when you're really just a mid-level developer. The wrong way of going about this would be to ask your manager if you could be promoted. Even worse would be to make a plan for learning the skills "required" to get the job you want!
"How do you learn such secrets?" you ask. Good question because that's the main topic of this blog. Of course, I will publish technical articles, but the most effective way to improve is not to learn some new framework or the XY language. That way you'd have to learn a million different things. Where would you even begin? But, learn to think like a one percenter and everything else will follow.
The skeptic in you might say "What is this guy trying to sell? He sounds like a get-rich-quick guru or something." Rest assured, the content on this blog is free and come from my own experience. In just 3 years, I 10xed my salary, teams consistently rate me as one of the best people they've worked with, and family life with my wife and son is better than ever.
